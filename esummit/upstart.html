Upstart

Are you a startup planning to be the next Flipkart? Do you aspire to be the star but are stuck being young in the field you have chosen? Here is an opportunity for you to improve upon that "million-dollar idea". Upstart is a competition aimed at providing up and coming startups, the guidance and mentorship of eminent leaders from the world of entrepreneurship. If your startup is new in the business and looking to pick up help in terms of funding, incubation or mentorship, Upstart is the platform you've been waiting for.

Every beginner seeks guidance from those who've already tread the path. Here is your chance to get that push your startup requires, to make an impactful presence in the world of entrepreneurship. Selected startups will get the chance to interact with a group of leading entrepreneurs, who'll analyse every aspect of your business and look at your startup with the eye of a critique as well as a mentor. We are offering you the chance, it’s up to you to grab it!

Competition Structure

Screening Round:

Submission of Executive Summary:
All participating startups have to submit an executive summary of their startups, only in the format described in the Next Section. The submissions will undergo a round of screening and 5 startups will be shortlisted for the Final Round Mentoring sessions, to be held in IIT Kanpur during eSummit'15.

Deadline for Screening Round Entries: 11:59 pm, 16 August, 2015


Final Round:

Intensive Mentoring Sessions:
Five shortlisted startups will have subsequent one to one mentoring and brainstorming sessions with a mentor from the panel of Upstart. Mentors will spend considerable amount of time exclusively on one start-up. Start-ups get to discuss their ideas and business plans to build a proper legal, operational and structural framework. Along with invaluable advice from one of the most experienced Entrepreneur-cum-Mentors in the country, startups also get to enhance their network manifold with help from them.

Final Pitch:
After the session, Startups will have time till the next day to build upon their Plan and modify their Pitch based on the inputs they received. They will then participate in a 10-minute pitch (followed by Q&As) to present their improved Business Plans before the Upstart panel, a panel of 5 judges. The start-ups will accordingly be given their final positions based on the Judging Criteria as elaborated in a later section.

Eligibility Criteria

As Upstart is a competition meant only for initial phase startups, we have put the following criteria for entry into the Final Round:

  • The Startup must have been incorporated at least, if not registered for operations.
  • The Startup’s date of incorporation should be on or after 1st September 2013.
  • The Startup should not have been VC funded. Startups financed through Bootstrapping or Seed Funding by Angel Investors are allowed to participate.
  • Startup entries must be the original work of participants i.e. team members competing must be the principal owners of the venture.

Judging Criteria

  1. Concept
    • Originality of Idea
    • Scalability, in terms of the potential for growth and replication of the venture.
    • Well-conceived implementation plan and timetable.
    • Overall likelihood of achieving demonstrable results

  2. Market
    • Evidence of demand for the venture in the marketplace; market size and growth opportunity.
    • Demonstrated knowledge of target market
    • Competitive analysis , SWOT analysis
    • Marketing Plan addresses (wherever applicable): positioning, pricing, advertising, promotion, packaging, timetable.

  3. Performance Benchmarks
    • Outline the development of systems to track, monitor and assess the performance milestones articulated in venture plan

  4. Financial Aspects
    • Fundability: The potential for the venture to receive financial support from additional investors/donors(including opportunities for funding from foundations, social venture funds, angel investors and venture capitalists)
    • The rigor and feasibility of the venture's financial model and goals
    • Potential for follow-on funding to grow the venture through self-sufficiency or profitability.

  5. Risk Assessment and Contingency Plans
    • Identify risk factors associated with the enterprise and external environment. Develop a plan for mitigating them.

  6. Demonstrated Leadership Ability
    • Quantify venture leader's ability to develop people, promote change and achieve results

  7. Improvement
    • Improvement in the business plan from original proposal, post the mentoring session, will be taken into consideration.




FAQ

Q. What should I do to participate in UPSTART'15?
A-> First, register for E-Summit. Once you are done with that, you can proceed to the submission on UPSTART page on the website. Submission of the Executive Summary counts as registration for the event

Q. What is the registration/participation fee for UPSTART'15?
A-> There is NO separate registration/participation fee.

Q. What is the last date for submission?
A-> The last date for submitting/editing your B-Plan is 16 August, 2015.

Q. How do I decide the category for my B-Plan?
A-> Refer to the rules and eligibility criteria of UPSTART'15.

Q. Can I submit any additional document that has not being asked for?
A-> No, we do not accept any other documents. The judging will be done strictly on the basis of documents which have been asked for.

Q. I have registered more than once for the same entry. What should I do?
A-> A single registration should be done for a single entry. If you have registered more than once, please send an e-mail to connect@ecelliitk.com, mentioning information about all the registrations made for the entry.

Q. How is confidentiality of the B-Plan maintained?
A-> The entries for UPSTART’15 are judged by reputed VC’s, established entrepreneurs and industrialists who believe in promoting entrepreneurship. Your entry is only viewed by the judge. At no stage is an organizer permitted to view your executive summary/ B-Plan. Presentations are closed door.

Q. What will be judging criteria?
A-> Refer to the Judging Criteria for UPSTART’15. Judging will be done by a group of reputed entrepreneurs, Venture Capitalists and experienced people from the industry.

Q. Does my business plan have to be technology specific?
A-> No, we encourage the entry of all well-developed business plans.

Q. How does the mentoring program work?
A-> Every startup qualifying the screening round will be assigned a mentor. The teams shall then have a brainstorming session with the assigned mentors wherein they can discuss any aspect of their business which demands guidance and help.

Q. When can I request for a Mentor?
A-> A mentor will be allotted to all the teams that qualify for the main round.
